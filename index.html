Sejarah Redvelvet

From a first perspective, red velvet is believed to have been around since the 1800s. At that time, the red velvet recipe was already known, but it still referred to a type of cake. This means that there is a cake known as red cake, which was later renamed red velvet because of the soft and tender texture of the cake (velvet).

Apart from that, redvelvet is also considered popular since World War II, namely around the 1940s. Red Velvet is known as a popular cake in the United States because it is a cake often served to soldiers at war. The reason red velvet was served to United States soldiers was quite simple.

During World War II, pastry chefs had difficulty getting the ingredients commonly used in making cakes, including food coloring. These limited conditions made chefs start to innovate and use natural dyes. More precisely, they use beets to make a red cake called red velvet.
